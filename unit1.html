Unit 1: Kinematics

The study of motion without considering its causes. Mastering 1D and 2D motion, projectile trajectories, and graphical analysis.

Key Concepts

Scalars vs. Vectors Difference between distance/displacement and speed/velocity.
Graphical Analysis Interpreting slope (velocity/acceleration) and area under the curve (displacement/change in velocity).
Free Fall Motion under the influence of gravity alone ($g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$).
Projectile Motion Independent horizontal (constant velocity) and vertical (accelerated) motions.

The "Big 3" Equations

These equations apply only when acceleration ($a$) is constant.

Velocity as a function of time

$$v_x = v_{x0} + a_x t$$

Position as a function of time

$$x = x_0 + v_{x0} t + \frac{1}{2}a_x t^2$$

Velocity independent of time

$$v_x^2 = v_{x0}^2 + 2a_x(x - x_0)$$

Unit Labs & Investigations

Standard Classroom Labs

These labs build the foundation for data collection and error analysis.


1. Constant Velocity (The Buggy Lab)

Goal: Determine the mathematical relationship between position and time for a battery-operated car.

  • Equipment: Meter stick, stopwatch, battery car.
  • Analysis: Plot Position ($x$) vs. Time ($t$). The slope represents the average velocity.
2. Accelerated Motion on a Ramp

Goal: Determine the acceleration of a cart rolling down an incline.

  • Analysis: Linearize data by plotting Position vs. Time squared ($t^2$) or Velocity vs. Time.
  • Result: Verify that $x \propto t^2$ for constant acceleration.
